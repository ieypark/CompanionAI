The Problem We Address

Loneliness among seniors is a growing public health challenge. Many existing solutions are expensive, clinically impersonal, or inaccessible to people with dementia or low digital literacy.

In Canada, more than 1.8 million seniors live alone and approximately 30% are at risk of social isolation. The consequences are not only emotional, but also associated with increased health risks.

  • Loneliness is associated with higher risk of cognitive decline and dementia.
  • It is associated with higher risk of cardiovascular events and reduced overall wellbeing.
  • Care settings often cannot provide spontaneous emotional engagement due to staffing constraints.
  • Families frequently experience guilt due to geographic distance and time limitations.

What CompanionAI Is

CompanionAI is not a monitoring tool or clinical device. It is a genuine companion that adapts to each person's unique history, preferences, and emotional needs.

Seniors do not need another system that observes them. They need to feel seen, remembered, and valued. CompanionAI offers spontaneous, voice-based companionship that is always patient, never rushed, and respectful of autonomy and dignity.

Natural daily conversation

Spontaneous voice conversations tailored to each person’s life story and preferences.

Gentle memory support

Reminiscence and cognitive engagement through storytelling rather than testing.

Family connection

Secure delivery of family messages in warm, familiar voice interactions.

Supportive wellness prompts

Friendly suggestions that feel like care, not alarms, to encourage daily routines.

Design intent: CompanionAI supports emotional wellbeing and connection. It does not diagnose, treat, or monitor medical conditions, and it is designed to complement, not replace, human care.
